User Guide

This guide explains how to use PharmaHub clearly and professionally. It is designed for pharmacy owners, admins, cashiers, and pharmacists who need to manage daily work inside the system with confidence.

Use this page as your everyday reference. Each section below explains what the feature does, when to use it, and how it helps you keep your pharmacy records accurate.
Getting Started

Login

The login page is the first step to entering the PharmaHub system. Every user must sign in with a valid username and password before gaining access to the pharmacy dashboard. This protects your pharmacy data and makes sure only authorized people can manage drugs, sales, purchases, and reports.

After entering your login details correctly, the system will take you to your dashboard based on your account role. If you forget your password, you should use the password reset option provided on the login page so you can securely recover access without creating confusion in staff records.

Tip: Always log out after using a shared system so that another person does not gain access to your account.
Overview

Dashboard

The dashboard is the main overview page of PharmaHub. It gives you a quick summary of important pharmacy information such as total drugs, low stock items, expired drugs, expiring soon products, daily sales, and daily purchases. This helps you understand the current state of your pharmacy without opening many pages.

Owners and staff should check the dashboard regularly because it acts like a control center for daily decisions. For example, if low stock increases or expired drugs appear, the dashboard immediately shows it, allowing you to restock or remove affected items before it impacts customers or pharmacy operations.

Tip: Use the dashboard at the beginning and end of each day to review pharmacy activity quickly.
Inventory

Drug Management

Drug management is where you create and maintain the pharmacy inventory. This section allows you to add new drugs, update existing drug details, and review important information such as category, batch number, quantity, unit price, expiry date, and manufacturer. Keeping this section accurate is very important because other parts of the system depend on it.

Before recording sales or purchases, make sure the drug already exists correctly in the system. When a drug record is updated properly, the pharmacy can track stock movement, expiry dates, and pricing more reliably. If the information here is incorrect, it can affect reports, alerts, and day-to-day stock control.

  1. Open the Drug List page.
  2. Click Add Drug to create a new record.
  3. Use Edit when you want to update an existing drug.
  4. Check quantity, price, and expiry details carefully before saving.
Tip: Always use clear drug names and the correct batch number so similar drugs do not get mixed up.
Transactions

Sales

The sales section is used to record every product sold in the pharmacy. When you add a sale, you usually select the drug, enter the customer name if needed, specify the quantity sold, and confirm the unit price. The system then calculates the total amount and reduces the available stock automatically.

Recording sales correctly is important because it affects both your stock balance and your business reports. If a sale is not entered, your stock may look higher than it really is. If a wrong quantity is entered, your reports and remaining inventory can become inaccurate. For this reason, sales should be recorded immediately after each transaction.

Tip: Review the drug selected and quantity entered before saving each sale to avoid stock errors.
Stock Control

Purchases

The purchases section is used whenever the pharmacy buys new stock from a supplier. This feature helps you record what drug was purchased, who supplied it, how many units were bought, and the purchase price. Once saved, the system increases stock automatically and keeps a record of the transaction for future reference.

Properly recording purchases helps the pharmacy maintain accurate inventory levels and understand how much money is being spent on restocking. It also makes it easier to compare stock coming in against stock going out through sales. Owners and admins should make sure purchases are entered promptly after receiving goods.

Tip: Enter purchases as soon as stock arrives so the quantity available in the system always matches the shelf.
Analytics

Reports

The reports section helps you review pharmacy activity in a more organized and professional way. PharmaHub provides stock reports, sales reports, purchase reports, and expiry reports. These reports allow you to understand inventory levels, monitor revenue, track expenses, and identify drugs that need attention.

Reports are useful for daily management, business reviews, and printed records. For example, the sales report helps you see how much revenue has been generated, while the stock report helps you confirm what is still available. The purchase report supports cost tracking, and the expiry report helps improve safety and compliance.

Tip: Use the print buttons on report pages whenever you need a hard copy for review or record keeping.
Monitoring

Alerts

The alerts section helps you notice important stock issues before they become serious problems. PharmaHub can highlight low stock items, expired drugs, and drugs that will expire soon. These alerts are designed to help the pharmacy react early instead of waiting until stock is finished or products become unsafe to sell.

This part of the system should be checked regularly because it supports safer and smarter inventory handling. Low stock alerts help you restock on time, while expiry alerts help you remove or manage affected drugs. Using these alerts consistently can reduce losses and improve the quality of service given to customers.

Tip: Check alerts daily so you can restock faster and separate expired products before they create risk.
Management

Staff & Subscription

The staff management section allows owners to create accounts for team members and assign the correct role to each person. Roles are important because they control what users can do inside the system. For example, some staff may only record sales, while owners and admins can manage wider pharmacy operations.

The subscription section allows the owner to view the current pharmacy plan, check subscription status, and upgrade using the available payment method. Keeping the subscription active is important because it ensures uninterrupted access to the system and its business features. Owners should review both staff access and subscription status regularly to keep operations smooth.

Tip: Give each staff member the correct role and review active subscriptions before the expiry date is reached.
